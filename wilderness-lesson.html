INTRODUCTION
IN CLASS

“Hello, everyone,” a cool voice said from the back of the room. Eoin entered, passing through the rows of students already seated and tittering.

“You should be in Wilderness Survival I. If that is not correct, this is your time to leave respectfully.” She looked around, waiting for anyone to depart. No one did, which was not a surprise. Beginning the class this way was more of an intimidation tactic than anything more functional.

She walked to the front of the tent, where a wide tree stump grew out of the ground next to an old wooden table. Eoin sat cross-legged atop the stump and looked around the room.

“You’ve all survived this far. Why do you need this class?” she asked. It was silent for a long moment.

“I asked a question. Why are you here?” she looked around, waiting for a student to answer.

One brave human student put a hand up. Eoin nodded at them, and they spoke quietly, looking at their desk, “Well, I know how to survive basically, so I don’t really need help knowing how to eat, or when to sleep. I’m hoping this class is going to teach me skills like making fires or tents, things I don’t know yet.”

Eoin smiled. “Beautiful, thank you. Does anyone else want to give it a try?”

Another student began to speak without raising a hand, but Eoin didn’t stop him. “I feel like I don’t even know what I don’t know, and I’m thinking you’re going to teach me.”

“Precisely,” Eoin nodded, stepping up from her seat on the stump. “One of the most basic things to learn about survival is knowing that you don’t know everything.

Once, I assumed a plant that looked similar to one I recognized was the same. I fed it to my cat, Milo, and we both spent the evening completely ill--

Knowing the limits of your knowledge is necessary to surviving. That’s what this is all about.”

She looked around. “It’s not all dark, I promise. If you stick around, I’ll teach you to build forts and climb trees and give your enemies awful rashes.”
